## Design Context

### Users
Church media and production teams use OBS Church Studio while actively running live services, sermons, worship sets, and lower-third cues. The dock is a narrow operational surface inside OBS, so users need fast scanning, minimal clicks, and dependable control under time pressure.

### Brand Personality
Professional, calm, operational. The product should feel trustworthy, focused, and production-ready rather than expressive or decorative.

### Aesthetic Direction
Compact broadcast control surfaces with solid dark/light neutrals, restrained accent color, and small-radius rectangular components. Surfaces should feel practical and implementation-real, with density driven by layout, spacing, and typography instead of visual effects.

### Design Principles
- Optimize for operational density first: reduce wasted space, keep controls aligned, and make scanning fast in narrow dock layouts.
- Use flat, grounded surfaces with subtle borders and minimal shadows; avoid gradients, glass, glow, and synthetic palettes.
- Keep geometry tight and mostly rectangular with very small radii so the dock reads like a real OBS tool.
- Build hierarchy through grouping, spacing, and typography before adding emphasis through color.
- Preserve readability under pressure: compact does not mean cramped, and primary actions must stay obvious.
